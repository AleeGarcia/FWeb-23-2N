The Scream

"The Scream" is one of the most iconic paintings in the world, created by the Norwegian artist Edvard Munch. The painting represents a figure with a horrified expression against a turbulent orange sky. The expression of fear and anxiety in the figure has made "The Scream" a symbol of existential dread and modern anguish.

Munch created several versions of "The Scream," using different mediums, but the essence of the work remains consistent. It is often interpreted as a depiction of the human condition in the modern world.

Artist: Edvard Munch

Year: 1893

Medium: Oil, tempera, pastel, and crayon on cardboard

Dimensions: 91 cm x 73.5 cm
